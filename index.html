In Hangul, consonants act as placeholders and have no sound when standalone

To remember which consonant is which, you can make associations with English words!

ㅇ no(thing)
ㅂ b(ucket)
ㄷ d(oor)
ㄱ g(un)
ㄹ r(attlesnake)
ㅁ m(ap)
ㄴ n(ose)
ㅍ p(art 2)
ㅅ s(ummit)


And here are two vowels:

ㅣ (tr)ee
ㅡ (br)oo(k)

In Hangul, syllables are letters squished into one word, like this:

이 eeh
비 bee
기 gi
리 ree
미 me
니 knee
피 pee
시 she
