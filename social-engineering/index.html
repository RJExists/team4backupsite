Spotting Fake Links

A real website will have the domain and extension (paypal.com) BEFORE any extras (/us/home).

Make sure any emails you interact with are from the correct domain!

Wrong domain extension:
https://www.microsoft.net

Malicious misspelling (cybersquatting):
https://www.microssoft.com

Lowercase ‘L’, uppercase ‘i', and vice versa:
https://www.waImart.com

Fake:
https://www.paypal.secure-site.com/us/home

Real:
https://www.paypal.com/us/home
https://www.copilot.microsoft.com/onboarding

Beware of Hyperlinks!

Hyperlinks are plain text that have a link embedded in them.

Hyperlinks are dangerous because they can mask a link's true destination.

DOs:

  • Go directly to the official website instead of clicking links.
  • Find an official phone number before calling. If called, hang up and find the official number.
  • Log into your account directly to check for unauthorized activity.
  • Call your loved ones to confirm any stories from an unknown number.
  • Keep your computer and antivirus up to date.

DON'Ts:

  • DON'T click on a bit.ly or cut.ly link from an unknown or untrusted source.
  • DON'T click on a link in an email or text message from an unknown or untrusted source.
  • DON'T call a phone number in an email or text message from an unknown or untrusted source.
  • DON'T buy gift cards on behalf of another person/strangers - they are very difficult to get a refund for.
  • DON'T make payments on the phone unless you know who you are talking to.

The government will NEVER call your phone without prior contact. If you’re in trouble, they know where to find you. Caution & education are the #1 defenses against social engineering!
